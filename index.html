Bio

I am a fifth year Agricultural & Resource Economics PhD candidate and an Innovations at the Nexus of Food, Energy, and Water Systems Fellow at University of California, Berkeley. I study questions related to how agricultural systems around the world interact with the environment, often using remote sensing and machine learning tools which I sometimes help develop. My current projects include quantifying the environmental benefits of conservation agriculture adoption in Mexico, re-examining the effects of China's Household Responsibility System using historical satellite imagery, and exploring how agricultural productivity shocks due to weather fluctuations affect civil conflict likelihood in general equilibrium.

Since starting my PhD at Berkeley, I have worked as a graduate student researcher for Solomon Hsiang, Elisabeth Sadoulet, the Berkeley Initiative for Transparency in the Social Sciences, David Zilberman, and Edward Miguel. Before coming to Berkeley I was a Stanford Law Empirical Research Fellow working for Daniel Kessler, I worked as a research assistant for Sean Higgins, and I earned a B.S. in Economics from Tulane University with minors in Mathematics and Portuguese.
